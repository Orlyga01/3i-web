The Miller-Urey Experiment (1953)
  • Scientists mixed water, hydrogen, ammonia, and methane, simulating early Earth's atmosphere, zapped it with electrical sparks, and shockingly produced amino acids, the building blocks of life, from scratch.
  • Panspermia: The theory that life's ingredients, or even life itself, can travel across the galaxy carried by objects like comets, asteroids, or interstellar visitors like 3I/ATLAS, potentially seeding life on other planets.
