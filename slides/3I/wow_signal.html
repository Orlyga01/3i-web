Small Bodies Overview

These three small-body types can look similar at first glance, but each one behaves differently near the Sun. This quick comparison sets up the comet-tail animation that comes next.

Asteroid

A mostly rocky body orbiting the Sun, often larger than a meteoroid and usually found in long-lasting orbital paths.

Comet

An icy and dusty object that can grow a glowing coma and tail when sunlight heats it near the Sun.

Meteoroid

A much smaller rocky or metallic piece traveling through space. If it enters Earth’s atmosphere, it can create a meteor.
